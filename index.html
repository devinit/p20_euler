Who are the global P20?

The P20 are the 1.4 billion men, women and children living in over 100 countries who are the poorest 20% of the global population. People in the P20 often experience multiple and intersecting deprivations, disadvantages and discrimination based on identity, circumstances and life events. The below visualisation enables you to explore how these experiences and identities intersect for the P20 across different countries.

About the P20 Initiative

The P20 Initiative focuses on the people who are in the poorest 20% of the global population. It aims to track their progress and improve information about their lives in order to ensure no one is left behind in efforts to tackle poverty.

Agenda 2030 and the Sustainable Development Goals have brought us a global commitment to end extreme poverty and ensure no one is left behind. For this ambition to be met, we need to know who the poorest people in the world are, understand their lives, and ensure they are included in progress.

Methodology

This visualisation is powered by data from Development Initiatives based on PovcalNet and selected DHS and MICS surveys. For detailed methodology notes please consult the annexes in the P20 baseline report.

The P20 Initiative promotes the production and use of disaggregated data. We believe, as a minimum standard, data should be disaggregated by quintile, geography, gender, age and disability (QGGAD) to ensure sufficient granularity to include everyone in progress. Of the data sources used to power this visualisation, there is no disability data available from PovcalNet. Only 11 of the 56 DHS surveys and 5 of the 41 MICS surveys in our sample have some questions about disability. Categories of people that are relatively small such as those experiencing a particular disability, are not represented well by this type of visualisation. Consequently, we have omitted disability data from this visualisation.

An unintentional overlap may appear when comparing binary indicators, such as male and female, urban and rural, or more than one age range. This is due to geometric constraints of the visualisation.

Data isn't available for all indicators for all countries, and due to data and geometric constraints, some combinations of indicators cannot be compared against each other.

Some indicators are only measured for certain population groups. Selecting 'Experience stunting' or 'Birth not registered' limits the selected population to children aged under 5 years. Selecting the education level limits the selected population to adults aged 25 years and older. Selecting 'Had no skilled attendant at birth' limits the selected population to girls and women aged 15 to 49 years.
